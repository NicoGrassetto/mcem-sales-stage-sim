# Microsoft Customer Engagement Methodology (MCEM)

## What is MCEM?

**MCEM** stands for **Microsoft Customer Engagement Methodology**.

It is Microsoft's unified framework for engaging customers across the entire lifecycle, designed to connect all customer-facing roles (sales, customer success, industry solutions, and partners) into one team. The goal is to deliver consistent, customer-centric engagements that drive business outcomes.

MCEM is central to **MCAPS (Microsoft Customer and Partner Solutions)** motions and is supported by detailed playbooks, orchestration guides, and role accountabilities.

---

## Purpose

Aligns Microsoft and partners around a common engagement model to:
- Improve customer experience
- Accelerate value realization
- Drive business outcomes
- Connect all customer-facing roles into one unified team

## ATU to STU Milestone Ownership Transition

### ATU (AE/ATS) Responsibilities
1. Engaging CxOs/Stakeholders across Rooms of the House to identify Customer Priorities
2. Creating and mapping Stage 1 Opptys to Customer Priorities and Stakeholders
3. Qualifying and transitioning Opptys to Stage 2 in collaboration with STU

### STU (Specialist) Responsibilities
1. Receiving and understanding qualified opportunity and supporting ATU in additional qualification if needed
2. Within 7 business days after transition discussion, taking ownership from ATU of Uncommitted milestones in MSX
3. Working with Customer to move Uncommitted milestones to Committed

### Stage 1 Opportunity Qualification Criteria

ATU and STU should collaborate to ensure Oppty is qualified, including:
- **Customer Outcomes** identified
- **Customer Decision maker/s** (business and technical) identified
- Identify major **technical blockers** and develop a plan to resolve them
- **Approval Process** determined and customer ready to engage in Stage 2
- **Budget** availability validated
- **Timing** confirmed

### Stage 1 Milestone Criteria
- ATU are expected to create an initial **Uncommitted Milestone** when they create a Stage 1 Consumption Opportunity in MSX
- Provision of **Milestone Est. Due date and Est. Value (not $0)** is required
- Focus on materiality of scope: Workloads, high-level sizing
- Reflect major timing/phasing constraints for commitment, deployment

### Required Handoff Process

> **REQUIRED:** ATU-to-STU Opportunity Handoff and Milestone transition discussion
> 
> ATU & SSP to agree on Milestone value and Due Date before Oppty is moved to Stage 2

---

## STU to CSU Consumption Opportunity Ownership Transition

### STU (Specialist/SE) Responsibilities
1. Committing the Consumption Opportunity at end of Stage 3 "Empower & Achieve" once customer has committed to proposed workloads, timeline and revenue is confirmed to have landed
2. Moving Opportunity (Consumption Intent) to Stage 4 "Realize Value" in MSX
3. Transitioning Consumption Opportunity to CSU

### CSU (CSA/CSAM) Responsibilities
1. Working with STU (SSP and SE) to confirm customer agreement details and any closing billed opportunity comments
2. Taking ownership of Consumption Opportunity in MSX from SSP when it moves to Stage 4 in MSX
3. Driving Consumption Opportunity to closure, updating status in MSX up to Est. Date

### Transition Timeline
- **SSP should transition ownership of Stage 4 Opportunity to CSU within 7 days**
- New owner will receive email notification when opportunity has been assigned to them in MSX

### Resources to Identify Opportunity Owners for Handoff
- Follow **Pipeline Walking decks** ‚Äì Azure/Usage
- **For Unified Accounts:** Check aka.ms/whoistheCSAM to identify the right CSAM owner for the committed Milestone
- **For Non-Unified Accounts:** Transition to the CSA manager accountable for your Area/OU

### Required Handoff Process

> **REQUIRED:** STU-to-CSU Opportunity Handoff and Milestone transition discussion (Azure)
> 
> STU (Specialist) & CSU (CSAM/CSA) should meet and validate Consumption Opportunity details before Opportunity ownership transferred to CSU

## Stage Outcomes and Activities

### Listen & Consult
**Customer Outcome:** Customer believes Microsoft could solve the problem
**Exit Criteria:** Qualified Opportunity

**Activities:**
- Consume Signals, Digital Qualification
- Listen & Qualify Opportunity
- Account Team and Partner Sync

### Inspire & Design
**Customer Outcome:** Customer has interest in proposed solution
**Exit Criteria:** Customer Aligned to Solution

**Activities:**
- Customer Workshops
- Technical Assessment & Show Technical Ability
- Business Value Assessment
- Solution Assessment
- Finalize Solution
- Create Customer Success Plan
- Finalize and Present Value Proposition

### Empower & Achieve
**Customer Outcome:** Prove business case and technology
**Exit Criteria:** Customer Agreement in Place

**Activities:**
- Technical and Business Proof
- Deal Shaping
- Negotiate Terms
- Process Contract

### Realize Value
**Customer Outcome:** Customer satisfied with implementation
**Exit Criteria:** Outcomes Met and Baseline Metrics in Place

**Activities:**
- Technical Implementation
- Change Management and Training
- Business Value Metric Baseline

### Manage & Optimize
**Customer Outcome:** Customer expectations are met
**Exit Criteria:** Opportunity Done and Next Steps Identified

**Activities:**
- Solution Health Achieved
- Business Value Metrics Achieved
- Rightsizing/Legacy Removal/Backlog Identification

## Role Definitions

### AE (Account Executive)
- **Focus:** Owns the overall account strategy and executive relationships
- Acts as the "quarterback" for the account
- Ensures alignment between business outcomes and Microsoft solutions

### ATS (Account Technology Strategist)
- **Focus:** Owns the technology and innovation strategy for the account
- Maps business value to technology capabilities
- Develops technical roadmaps and secures technical agreements

### SSP (Solution Sales Professional)
- **Focus:** Drives solution-specific sales motions
- Qualifies opportunities and executes Solution Plays
- Secures the business decision for the solution area

### SE (Solution Engineer)
- **Focus:** Technical assessment and architecture
- Delivers initial architecture and proof of concepts
- Secures technical decisions

### CSA (Cloud Solution Architect)
- **Focus:** Technical delivery and consumption
- Drives implementation and usage for committed milestones
- Ensures solution health and identifies expansion opportunities

### CSAM (Customer Success Account Manager)
- **Focus:** Orchestrates customer success and value realization
- Creates and manages the Customer Success Plan
- Coordinates delivery and ensures long-term value

### CE (Commercial Executive)
- **Focus:** Deal strategy and execution
- Drives negotiation and deal execution at the commercial level

### SAE (Services Account Executive)
- **Focus:** Services opportunity qualification and delivery
- Orchestrates Services teams and identifies services expansion

### PSS/PDM (Partner Solution Specialist / Partner Development Manager)
- **Focus:** Partner engagement and co-sell motions
- When PSS is not in place, PDM is accountable
